Liquidation Map
Each bar is a price level with leveraged positions stacked on it. The wider the bar, the more USD is at risk of forced liquidation at that price.
Pink bars (above price)
Short positions. Price rising into these triggers their liquidation, injecting cascading buy orders into the market.
Green bars (below price)
Long positions. Price falling into these forces liquidation, injecting cascading sell orders into the market.
Large clusters act like magnets — price tends to move toward them because market makers hunt that liquidity. Watch for the biggest $ level nearest to current price.
OI Flow
Open Interest = total $ value of all open contracts. OI Flow shows how it changed every 5 minutes.
OI rising + price rising — new longs opening, bullish
OI rising + price falling — new shorts opening, bearish
OI falling + price rising — shorts squeezed/closed, momentum could fade
OI falling + price falling — longs closing in panic, potential capitulation
AI Signals
L/S Ratio — what % of traders are long vs short. Above 60%L = crowd over-extended long, contrarian bearish. Below 40%L = crowd over-short, contrarian bullish.
Taker Flow — who is being aggressive: buyers or sellers. Takers cross the spread and move price. High buy taker flow = market buyers in control.
Funding — positive = longs paying shorts (market leans bullish). Negative = shorts paying longs (market leans bearish). Extreme positive funding often precedes a correction.
OI Trend — short-term direction of open interest. Negative = positions being closed (deleveraging). Watch for divergence with price.
24h Price — session context. If signals are bearish but 24h is already −5%, a relief bounce is more likely than continuation.
